From September 2021, new teachers in England must complete a 2-year induction after they have done their initial teacher training (ITT).

The induction must be based on the early career framework (ECF) developed by the Department for Education (DfE), teachers, school leaders, academics and experts.

It will help teachers develop the knowledge, practices and working habits that set them up for a successful teaching career. They will work closely with a mentor throughout their induction.

A range of free online induction materials are available, and the DfE will fund cover for:

  • 10% of time teachers and mentors spend away from the classroom in the first year of induction
  • 5% in the second year

Teachers will study 8 areas during their induction:

  • setting high expectations
  • helping pupils progress
  • demonstrating good subject and curriculum knowledge
  • planning and teaching well-structured lessons
  • adapting to the different needs of pupils
  • making accurate and productive use of assessments
  • managing behaviour in the classroom
  • developing professional skills such as building relationships and communicating effective

How schools can run their induction programmes
