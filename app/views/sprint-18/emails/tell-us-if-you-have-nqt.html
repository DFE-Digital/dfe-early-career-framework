From: Department for Education: Teacher Continuing Professional Development

Subject: Tell us if you have NQTs joining in 2021

Hello,

The Department for Education (DfE) needs some important information from ((school name)) as soon as possible.

You need to tell us if you do or do not expect to have any early career teachers (also known as NQTs) joining you in 2021.

Use this link to tell us now:
https://manage-training-for-early-career-teachers.education.gov.uk/nominations/start?token=22345678as9fijhgt

(Do not share this link. It will only work for your school).

What happens next

If you answer ‘yes’ or you do not know - you’ll be asked to nominate someone at your school to set up your statutory induction programme. We’ll let them know how to do this.

If you answer ‘no’ - you will not receive any more messages about statutory inductions for early career teachers until the next academic year.

Why we need this information now

From September 2021, statutory induction is changing. The ‘NQT year’ will become a 2-year induction programme for early career teachers (ECTs).

We’ve created an online service to help you access the support offered by the DfE and set up your programme.

If you have ECTs joining you in 2021, you need to nominate someone to access the service and get your programme set up as soon as possible.

How to get more guidance

Find out more about the statutory changes and how your school can set up an induction:

https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/early-career-framework-reforms-overview/early-career-framework-reforms-overview

You can contact your local teaching school hub for help:

https://www.gov.uk/guidance/teaching-school-hubs#find-a-teaching-school-hub

You can also contact your MAT (multi-academy trust) or appropriate body for guidance.

If you have any questions, reply to this email.
