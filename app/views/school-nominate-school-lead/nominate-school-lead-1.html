From: Department for Education: Teacher Continuing Professional Development

Subject: Set up your NQT induction (Early Career Framework)

[school name],

From September 2021, statutory induction is changing. The ‘NQT year’ will become a 2 year induction programme for early career teachers (ECTs).

These programmes must be based on the early career framework (ECF), designed to help these new teachers develop a successful career.

There are 3 approaches schools can choose from. These are compliant with the statutory changes:

  1. A training provider leads the programme, funded by the Department for Education (DfE).
  2. The school delivers their own programme using DfE accredited materials.
  3. The school designs and delivers their own programme based on the ECF.

We’ve created an online service to help schools access the support offered by the Department of Education (DfE) and set up their programme.

What schools can do now

If you are expecting any early career teachers in September, nominate your induction tutor.

An ‘induction tutor’ is someone at your school who usually manages statutory inductions for early career teachers.

They coordinate the training, support and assessment these new teachers receive. If you think this is your role, you can nominate yourself.

Nominate your induction tutor:

https://manage-training-for-early-career-teachers.education.gov.uk/nominations/start?token=22345678as9fijhgt

(Do not share this link. It is set up for your school only)

When do schools need to do this?

Schools should nominate an induction tutor as soon as possible, so they receive updates directly.

Induction tutors need to set up their programme before their early career teachers start their induction.

For example, if they are starting in September 2021, these steps must be completed by then.

Where to get more information

We have published guidance on:

  • the new statutory changes
  • the 3 approaches schools can take to run their induction
  • how programmes are funded and more

https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/early-career-framework-reforms-overview/early-career-framework-reforms-overview

Schools can also contact their local Teaching School Hub or appropriate body. They can give you more information on the different approaches.

Not sure? Find your nearest hub:

https://www.gov.uk/guidance/teaching-school-hubs#find-a-teaching-school-hub

If you are part of a MAT (multi academy trust), you may also want to consult with them.
