From: Department for Education: Teacher Continuing Professional Development

Subject: Important: NQT induction changes

[school name],

From September 2021 statutory induction is changing. Schools must offer their new early career teachers (ECTs) a 2 year induction programme.

The Department for Education (DfE) has provided:

  • statutory guidance so that schools know their roles and responsibilities
  • guidance on the 3 programmes that are compliant with the new regulations:
    1. an approved training provider leads the programme
    2. the school delivers the programme using DfE accredited materials
    3. schools design and deliver their own programme based on the early career framework (ECF)

Schools need to read our guidance and choose an approach that works for them:

https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/early-career-framework-reforms-overview/early-career-framework-reforms-overview

What schools can do next

We have launched an online service to help schools manage their new programme.

When your school has decided on an approach, nominate someone who manages statutory inductions at your school:

https://manage-training-for-early-career-teachers.education.gov.uk/nominations/start?token=22345678as9fijhgt
